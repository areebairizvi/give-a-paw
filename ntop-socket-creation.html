Latticing¶

Our custom nTop block converts the solid socket shell into a lattice. Three parameters drive how the lattice looks and behaves: point count, boundary lattice thickness, and surface lattice thickness. Each slider below isolates one parameter.

Point Count

The point count controls how densely the lattice is sampled across the surface: a low count yields a sparse, open structure that flexes more freely; a high count yields a finer, denser mesh that is stiffer and distributes load over more contact area. Drag the slider to see how the same socket changes as the point count steps from 5 up to 500.

Boundary Lattice Thickness

The boundary lattice thickness controls how thick the strands are along the outer edge of the lattice. Thicker boundaries give a more rigid rim and a defined silhouette; thinner boundaries blend into the surface lattice and flex more.

Surface Lattice Thickness

The surface lattice thickness controls how thick each strand of the lattice itself is. Thicker strands give a heavier, stiffer socket with smaller open cells; thinner strands give a lighter, more compliant socket with more open structure.
